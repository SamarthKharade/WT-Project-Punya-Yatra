Discover the Beauty of Maharashtra

Join us as we explore the hidden gems, cultural heritage, and breathtaking landscapes of Maharashtra.

Gateway of India

The Gateway of India in Mumbai is an iconic landmark overlooking the Arabian Sea. Built in 1924, it represents India's colonial past and vibrant present.

Ajanta Caves

A UNESCO World Heritage site, the Ajanta Caves in Chhtrapati SambhajiNagar feature stunning rock-cut Buddhist temples dating back to the 2nd century BCE.

Ellora Caves

Ellora Caves, another UNESCO site, showcase a mix of Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist rock-cut temples. The Kailasa Temple, carved from a single rock, is a masterpiece of ancient architecture.

Mahabaleshwar

Mahabaleshwar, a hill station in the Western Ghats, is famous for its strawberry farms, scenic viewpoints, and lush greenery. It is an ideal getaway for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Lonavala

Lonavala is known for its misty mountains, waterfalls, and historical forts. Popular attractions include Bhushi Dam, Rajmachi Fort, and the beautiful Karla and Bhaja Caves.

Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort, once the capital of Chhtrapati Shivaji Maharaj, offers breathtaking views and rich Maratha history. A trek to the fort is both thrilling and rewarding for history buffs and adventure seekers.

Tadoba National Park

Tadoba National Park, Maharashtra's oldest and largest wildlife sanctuary, is home to majestic tigers and diverse wildlife. A jungle safari here is a must for nature lovers.
